SaveVision01
The problem

The scarce resource isn't hands — it's judgment

  • In trauma, most preventable deaths happen in the first minutes — massive bleeding, blocked airway.
  • Trained medics are scarce; evacuation is delayed.
  • Often only an untrained bystander is there, not knowing what to do — and time is lost.
Trained judgment can travel "over a wire" even when a medic can't arrive in time.
SaveVision02
The solution

You see what the casualty sees

  • A bystander in smart glasses streams their first-person view (POV) to a remote doctor.
  • You guide them — by voice, drawings, photos and a map on the display — until help arrives.
  • Data is one-way: the doctor decides, the bystander acts.
SaveVision03
How it works

Call → You see → You guide

1

Call

From the phone or the glasses, the bystander reaches the operations centre. The glasses POV links automatically.

2

You see

A licensed doctor watches the live POV, sees the location on a map and the ambulance ETA.

3

You guide

Instructions, drawings on the wound, reference photos, a map — and you approve AI proposals before they're sent.

SaveVision04
Doctor's interface

What you see as the operator

  • Live POV of the casualty + a drawing tool over the image.
  • Mini-map with the casualty's location and the ambulance ETA.
  • Call queue by severity: critical / urgent / stable.
  • Task assignment — to people on scene and to operators.
SaveVision05
Capabilities

What you send to the display

  • Instruction text — a banner on the display.
  • Freehand drawing — mark exactly where to apply a tourniquet or pressure.
  • Reference photo / diagram — tourniquet, CPR, recovery position.
  • Map / direction — a real map, collection point, route.

SaveVision07
Safety & control

A human makes every decision

  • One-way: nothing auto-sends — you approve every step.
  • AI only proposes (wound-image analysis) — the decision is always the doctor's.
  • End-to-end encrypted (E2E) over Matrix, self-hosted — no third-party cloud.
  • A medical-only channel — no militarily useful information.
SaveVision08
IMPORTANT
Limits

What it is not

  • Not a replacement for emergency medical care and evacuation — it's decision-support that buys minutes.
  • Only non-invasive actions a layperson can perform under guidance.
  • Scene safety first: the system tells the bystander to withdraw if it's unsafe.
  • Outcome not guaranteed, even with flawless guidance.
SaveVision09
Legal / IHL

Protection of the wounded

  • The wounded and those who help them are protected under International Humanitarian Law.
  • The discipline of the medical channel preserves the bystander's civilian status.
  • Informed consent, scope of practice, the "do no harm" principle.

Use cases

Scenarios

  • Massive limb haemorrhage — guided tourniquet.
  • Unconscious casualty — breathing check, recovery position.
  • Burns, fractures, shock, childbirth, mass casualties — triage by a single bystander.
  • Beyond medicine: evacuation, search & rescue, technical guidance.
